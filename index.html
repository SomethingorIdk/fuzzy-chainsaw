The Structure of a Piano Concerto

A piano concerto is a composition for piano accompanied by an orchestra. It usually has three movements.

  • 1st movement: A moderately fast movement in sonata-allegro form, usually including a virtuosic cadenza.
  • 2nd movement: A slow movement that is more expressive and lyrical, usually in ternary form.
  • 3rd movement: A faster rondo.
